Section 1: The library contains rare books and manuscripts

The Library of congress contains thousands of rare books and manuscripts for you to explore. They have small and large books. They have examples of old style printing. They also have amazing sections such as the Gutenberg Bible and Presidential Papers.

Section 2: Does the Library offer books for the blind?

The answer is yes the library does offer books for the blind! They offer a wide selection of sound recordings, as well as braille books. This makes it easy and accessible for the blind to read the books in the library.

Section 3: Does the Library have comic books?

Yes! The Library actually has the record for the world's largest collection of comic books! Isn't that cool? Here is a list of a few comic books you can find at the Library.

  • Superman
  • Zap
  • Detective Comics

Section 4: The library of Congress is the largest Library in the United States!

It has millions of items including books, newspapers, comic books, audio, braille, maps, manuscripts, music and much more. There is something for everyone!

Section 5: Has the library ever been vandalized?

Yes, unfortunately the library was destroyed when British troops razed the building it was housed in, stealing many books and destroying many others.

Although this tradegy occured, Thomas Jefferson donated his own collection of more than 6,000 books to the congress for the purpose of restoring the library. He was compensated nearly 24,000 dollars for this donation.
